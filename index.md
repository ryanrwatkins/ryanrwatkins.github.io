[Full CV](cv.md) \| [Bios](bios.md) \| [GWU Website](https://gsehd.gwu.edu/directory/ryan-watkins) \| [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=heVXyrAAAAAJ&hl=en) \| [GitHub Repos](https://github.com/ryanrwatkins)

## Current Focus

My current work focuses on trustworthy AI, human-technology collaboration, educational technology, and the ethical use of large language models in scientific research and teaching workflows. I study how people, organizations, and institutions define useful results, make decisions with emerging technologies, and design systems that better serve human needs.

## Selected Work

* Associate Director, GW Trustworthy AI.
* Professor in Educational Technology Leadership and Program Director for the Human-Technology Collaboration Ph.D. program at George Washington University.
* Author and co-author of work on large language models in research workflows, needs-aware artificial intelligence, human needs and AI, and AI's influence on human creative agency.
* Contributor to open educational resources and development guides, including *Teaching and Learning with Jupyter*, *A Guide to Assessing Needs*, and multiple World Bank knowledge exchange guides.
* Developer of research and learning platforms including [Synthetic Prediction Markets Platform](https://pmp.weshareresearch.com), [PreprintScout](https://pypi.org/project/preprintscout/), [We Share Research](https://weshareresearch.com), and [We Share Science](https://wesharescience.com).
* Co-host and producer of the Parsing Science podcast.
